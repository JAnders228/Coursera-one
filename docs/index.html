Tagliatelle

Tagliatelle are long, flat, ribbon-like pasta, originating from Emilia-Romagna, Italy.They are traditionally made with eggs, giving them a rich, slightly yellow color. Tagliatelle are best known for their iconic pairing with Bolognese sauce, creating a classic Italian masterpiece. The pasta's long, flat shape allows it to perfectly intertwine with the meaty sauce, resulting in a satisfying and flavorful bite.

Rigatoni

Rigatoni are short, tubular pasta with distinctive ridges running along their length. These ridges provide excellent surface area for capturing sauces, making them ideal for hearty dishes. Classics like Amatriciana, Carbonara, and Gricia shine when paired with rigatoni. The pasta's sturdy structure holds up well to robust sauces, and its hollow center adds a delightful textural dimension.

Gnocchi

Gnocchi are light and fluffy dumplings, typically made from potatoes, flour, and sometimes ricotta cheese. Their origins trace back to ancient Roman times, evolving from simple doughs to the diverse regional variations we know today. Gnocchi are incredibly versatile, excelling with simple sauces like tomato sauce, pesto, or brown butter and sage. Their soft texture makes them a comforting and satisfying meal, perfect for a cozy evening.
